What Should a Salesperson Do in the AI Era?

Author: Axiom Methodology Team · June 2026

AI writes our emails, fills our CRM databases, scores our leads, and even summarizes our meeting notes. While the sales industry is facing the largest automation wave in history, one question comes to mind: So, what is left for the salesperson to do?

For decades, traditional sales trainings brought up salespeople as "information transmitters" or "process trackers." Operational tasks such as explaining product features to the client, preparing price quotes, and sending follow-up emails took up almost 70% of a salesperson's time. Today, all of these tasks can be completed by AI in seconds, with zero errors.

1. Shifting from Operational Roles to Value Creation

In this new paradigm, there will be no room for salespeople who only track processes and transmit information. However, this is not the end of the sales profession; on the contrary, it is the beginning of its most qualified era. While AI frees the salesperson from operational chores, it creates space for them to use their most important competency: building human relationships and establishing trust.

"A client never buys from an algorithm. The client buys from the human who truly understands their problem, empathizes with them, and shares their risk."

2. Working with the AI Multiplier: E = Y × AI²

The formula we developed at Axiom applies to the field of sales as well. Without a salesperson's core competency (Y)—such as active listening, empathy, complex problem-solving, and negotiation skills—giving them the best AI tools in the world is useless. AI multiplied by zero competency will still yield a zero result.

However, when you multiply a high-competency salesperson by AI, the generated impact quadruples. The salesperson can:

  • Conduct deep sectoral analysis of the client via AI in just 5 minutes before the meeting,
  • Prepare for client objections using simulated AI scenarios,
  • Automate email traffic and CRM updates after meetings.

3. 3 Critical Steps for the Future Salesperson

Corporate sales teams that want to survive and multiply their performance in the new era must realize these three transformations:

  1. Develop Analytical Competency: They must be able to read the data analyses provided by AI and offer the client data-driven business insights, not just product descriptions.
  2. Prioritize Negotiation and Empathy: Place human connection, which is the only thing that cannot be automated, at the center of their work.
  3. Position AI Tools as Assistants: Accept AI not as a threat, but as a cyber assistant that grants operational speed.

Consequently, AI will not replace salespeople. However, salespeople who use AI will rapidly replace those who do not.
